## ☀️ Solar Teaching Kit

Welcome to the Solar Teaching Kit! We are excited to support you in bringing hands-on solar energy education into your classroom.

> ⚠️ **Please complete Steps 1–3 a bit in advance** to ensure the Teaching Kit is ready before your lesson.

---

### 🔧 Step 1: Preparing your Kit

To begin, please collect all required materials to assemble your Solar Teaching Kit. You can view the complete Bill of Materials and the required 3D-printable components at the following links:

- 🔗 [View Bill of Materials on Thingiverse](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:7093153)
- 🔗 [View Potibox on Thingiverse](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:7093158)

---

### 🛠️ Step 2: Assembling the Kit

If you already have all components available, you may proceed with the assembly. Please refer to the Teacher Workbook for step-by-step instructions.

---

### 🧰 Step 3: Supporting Tools & Kit Setup

In addition to the main kit parts, gather all basic classroom tools needed for the experiments. Please refer to the Teacher Workbook and review the section "What is needed for this?" for a full materials checklist.

---

### 📘 Step 4: Teachers Workbook with solutions

To support your classroom instruction, we offer a Teacher Workbook including solutions.

- 📄 [Teacher Workbook with Exercises (PPTX)](https://edu-grid.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/TeachersWorksheets.pptx)

---

### 🎓 Step 5: Download the Classroom Presentation

To support your lesson delivery, you can download the PowerPoint presentations below designed for classroom use:

- 🔗 [Download Classroom PowerPoint — Chapter 1.1: PV Basics](https://edu-grid.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/PV-Education-HUB_Chapter-1.1_PV-Basics.pptx)
- 🔗 [Download Classroom PowerPoint — Chapter 1.2: Connecting PV to a Load & MPP](https://edu-grid.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/PV-Education-HUB_Chapter-1.2_Connecting-PV-to-a-load-and-MPP.pptx)
